<h2><a href="https://leetcode.com/problems/maximum-bitwise-xor-after-rearrangement">Maximum Bitwise XOR After Rearrangement</a></h2> <img src='https://img.shields.io/badge/Difficulty-Medium-orange' alt='Difficulty: Medium' /> <hr> <p>You are given two binary strings <code>s</code> and <code>t</code>, each of length <code>n</code>.</p> <p>You may rearrange the characters of <code>t</code> in any order, but <code>s</code> must remain unchanged.</p> <p>Return a binary string of length <code>n</code> representing the <strong>maximum integer value</strong> obtainable by taking the bitwise XOR of <code>s</code> and rearranged <code>t</code>.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <p><strong class="example">Example 1:</strong></p> <pre> <strong>Input:</strong> s = "101", t = "011" <strong>Output:</strong> "110" <strong>Explanation:</strong> One optimal rearrangement of t is "011". "101" XOR "011" = "110", which is the maximum possible. </pre> <p><strong class="example">Example 2:</strong></p> <pre> <strong>Input:</strong> s = "0110", t = "1110" <strong>Output:</strong> "1101" <strong>Explanation:</strong> One optimal rearrangement of t is "1011". "0110" XOR "1011" = "1101", which is the maximum possible. </pre> <p><strong class="example">Example 3:</strong></p> <pre> <strong>Input:</strong> s = "0101", t = "1001" <strong>Output:</strong> "1111" <strong>Explanation:</strong> One optimal rearrangement of t is "1010". "0101" XOR "1010" = "1111", which is the maximum possible. </pre> <p>&nbsp;</p> <p><strong>Constraints:</strong></p> <ul> <li><code>1 &lt;= n == s.length == t.length &lt;= 2 * 10<sup>5</sup></code></li> <li><code>s[i]</code> and <code>t[i]</code> are either <code>'0'</code> or <code>'1'</code>.</li> </ul>
